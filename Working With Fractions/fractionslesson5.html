Fractions Lesson 5: Mixed Fractions & Improper Fractions

Mixed Numbers have whole numbers and a fraction associated with them.

This Mixed Number example means there are 3 whole parts and one-third of a whole part.

Improper Fractions are fractions in which the numerator is equal to or greater than the denominator.

The improper fraction on the left means that there is more than one whole (13/4).

The improper fraction on the right means there is one whole but represented as a fraction (4/4).

To convert a Mixed Number to an improper fraction: Multiply the whole number by the denominator of the fraction.

Add the numerator to this product (this will be the new numerator for the improper fraction). Set the original denominator as the new denominator.
