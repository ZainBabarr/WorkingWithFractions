Fractions Lesson 2: Adding & Subtracting Fractions

Adding and subtracting fractions involve finding a common denominator so that the two fractions can be added or subtracted.

To add or subtract fractions, we first need to make sure the denominators are the same.

We cannot just add any two fractions because fractions represent parts of a whole, and each fraction can have a different number of equal parts.

When we add or subtract fractions, we are essentially trying to combine different numbers of equal parts into a new fraction.



To find the lowest common denominator, first identify the denominators of the fractions.

Then, find the least common multiple of these denominators.

Finally, use this least common multiple as the lowest common denominator for the fractions.



Once a common denominator is found, then the numerators can be added / subtracted.
